# DPA + MSA Fallback Clauses (Commercial Fastlane)

> Purpose: Provide fallback language blocks to reduce legal cycle time when a buyer paper introduces non-standard terms.  
> Owner: Legal (required approver)  
> Usage policy: Sales/CS may propose these clauses, but only Legal can approve final language.

## 1) Mutual Confidentiality (Fallback)

- Each party protects Confidential Information using at least reasonable care.
- Use is limited to performing obligations under the agreement.
- Exclusions: publicly available, independently developed, lawfully received from a third party, or already known without restriction.
- Compelled disclosure requires prompt notice when legally permitted.

## 2) Data Processing Scope (DPA Fallback)

- Provider acts as processor/service provider for customer personal data processed on customer instructions.
- Processing purpose is limited to delivering contracted services and documented support/security operations.
- Categories of data, data subjects, and processing activities are defined in Annex A of the DPA.
- Provider assists customer with data subject rights and breach notifications as required by law.

## 3) Security Measures Clause (Fallback)

- Provider maintains an information security program aligned to industry-standard controls.
- Baseline controls include access management, encryption, logging/monitoring, vulnerability management, and incident response.
- Provider may update security controls provided no material reduction in overall security posture.

## 4) Subprocessor Clause (Fallback)

- Provider may engage subprocessors with written agreements imposing materially equivalent privacy/security obligations.
- Current subprocessors are listed in a maintained notice location (`[[insert URL]]`).
- Provider will notify customer of material subprocessor changes per notice period in the DPA.
- Customer may object on reasonable data-protection grounds; parties will work in good faith on remediation.

## 5) International Transfer Clause (Fallback)

- Where required, parties adopt an approved transfer mechanism (for example, SCCs or UK IDTA/addendum).
- Supplemental safeguards are applied based on transfer-risk assessment and applicable law.

## 6) Security Incident Notification (Fallback)

- Provider will notify customer without undue delay after confirming a Security Incident affecting customer data.
- Notice includes known details, impact scope, remediation actions, and follow-up updates as materially new facts emerge.
- Notifications are sent to designated customer contacts in the order form or security exhibit.

## 7) Audit & Assurance Clause (Fallback)

- Provider supplies summary audit/compliance materials under NDA.
- If required by law/regulation, parties may agree to a scoped audit process that avoids unreasonable disruption.
- Customer bears audit costs unless a material non-compliance is identified.

## 8) Liability-Carve Coordination Clause (Fallback)

- DPA liability follows the negotiated MSA liability framework unless required otherwise by non-waivable law.
- No clause expands liability beyond explicit negotiated caps except where legally mandated.

## 9) Data Return/Deletion on Termination (Fallback)

- Upon request, provider returns customer data in a commonly used format within agreed timeline.
- Provider deletes remaining customer data after return period, except legally required retention.
- Backup systems follow standard purge lifecycle and technical constraints.

## 10) SLA Reference Clause (Fallback)

- Service levels and support commitments are governed by the SLA/SLO sheet in effect at contract signature.
- Service credits (if any) are customer’s sole and exclusive remedy for SLA breaches, unless otherwise stated in the MSA.

## 11) Governing Law / Venue (Balanced Fallback)

- Parties may use neutral governing law/venue where both sides operate in multiple jurisdictions.
- Alternative: customer paper for public sector/regional constraints subject to commercial and risk review.

## 12) Short “Fallback Pack” Snippets for Redlines

- **Security program commitment:** “Provider shall maintain a written information security program with administrative, technical, and organizational safeguards appropriate to the Services.”
- **Subprocessor flow-down:** “Provider will bind subprocessors to written obligations no less protective than those in this DPA.”
- **Incident notice:** “Provider will notify Customer without undue delay after confirming a Security Incident affecting Customer Data.”
- **Deletion commitment:** “Upon termination and request, Provider will return or delete Customer Data, subject to legal retention obligations.”

## 13) Required Legal Checks Before Use

- Confirm fallback language does not conflict with current template MSA/DPA baseline.
- Confirm transfer mechanism language matches customer jurisdiction.
- Confirm no mismatch between SLA remedy terms and MSA limitation/remedy clauses.
- Record approved redline set in the deal room before sending to counterparty.
