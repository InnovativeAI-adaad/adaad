# ADAAD Certification Program

## Program Purpose
The ADAAD Certification Program establishes role-aligned credentials that validate practical capability in governed AI operations, constitutional controls, and enterprise-scale platform administration.

The three certification levels are:

1. **Operator Associate** — entry-level operational fluency for day-to-day governed execution.
2. **Governance Engineer** — implementation-level competency for policy, controls, and deterministic governance automation.
3. **Enterprise Administrator** — organization-wide authority for architecture, risk posture, and cross-team governance lifecycle management.

---

## Certification Level 1: Operator Associate

### Candidate Profile
Operator Associates are analysts, technical operators, and support personnel who execute standard workflows in governed environments.

### Learning Objectives
By the end of preparation, candidates should be able to:

1. Explain ADAAD operating principles, fail-closed behavior, and immutable governance constraints.
2. Navigate operational interfaces, event logs, and evidence artifacts to validate routine tasks.
3. Execute approved runbooks for intake, verification, and controlled promotion in non-critical lanes.
4. Identify and escalate governance gate failures using structured incident communication.
5. Demonstrate baseline security hygiene for credentials, access control, and audit traceability.

### Exam Blueprint
| Domain | Weight | Skills Measured |
|---|---:|---|
| ADAAD Foundations & Terminology | 20% | Core concepts, control lanes, phase sequencing, governance vocabulary |
| Operational Workflows | 30% | Standard intake-to-stage operations, required checks, operator handoffs |
| Governance Gate Awareness | 25% | Gate purpose, failure interpretation, escalation criteria |
| Security & Audit Discipline | 15% | Least privilege, token handling, log integrity, evidence retention |
| Incident Triage & Communication | 10% | Structured reporting, severity classification, handoff quality |

**Assessment Format**
- 60 selected-response questions
- 90 minutes
- Passing score: 72%
- Recommended prerequisite: onboarding + 20 hours guided practice

### Practical Labs
1. **Lab OA-1: Controlled Intake and Validation**
   - Execute a standard intake workflow.
   - Verify required metadata and governance schema validity.
   - Produce an intake validation summary artifact.
2. **Lab OA-2: Gate Failure Escalation Drill**
   - Diagnose a simulated Tier 0/Tier 1 failure.
   - Document impact, root cause hypothesis, and escalation path.
   - Submit operator-grade incident handoff.
3. **Lab OA-3: Audit Trail Reconstruction**
   - Reconstruct a historical operation from logs and evidence matrix entries.
   - Confirm whether all required controls were satisfied.

**Lab Pass Criteria**
- Completion of all required steps
- Zero unresolved critical errors
- Evidence artifact quality score >= 80/100

## Certification Level 2: Governance Engineer

### Candidate Profile
Governance Engineers design, implement, test, and verify control logic and enforcement paths across policy-sensitive surfaces.

### Learning Objectives
By the end of preparation, candidates should be able to:

1. Implement policy enforcement logic while preserving deterministic behavior and replay integrity.
2. Map constitutional and architecture constraints into testable controls.
3. Build and maintain governance-aware CI gate stacks with evidence-complete outputs.
4. Diagnose and remediate policy drift, replay divergence, and control regressions.
5. Author high-quality governance documentation and release evidence suitable for audit.

### Exam Blueprint
| Domain | Weight | Skills Measured |
|---|---:|---|
| Governance Architecture & Invariants | 20% | Constraint hierarchy, invariant enforcement, control boundaries |
| Determinism & Replay Assurance | 20% | Deterministic design patterns, replay validation, divergence handling |
| Testing & CI Gate Engineering | 25% | Tiered gate strategy, governance tests, artifact verification |
| Secure Implementation Patterns | 20% | Fail-closed coding, import boundaries, security-aware interfaces |
| Evidence, Audit, and Change Governance | 15% | Claims-evidence mapping, release completeness, change traceability |

**Assessment Format**
- 75 selected-response questions
- 2 hands-on scenario sections
- 150 minutes total
- Passing score: 78%
- Recommended prerequisite: Operator Associate + 6 months governance implementation exposure

### Practical Labs
1. **Lab GE-1: Invariant Enforcement Build**
   - Implement a constrained policy check with deterministic output.
   - Add targeted tests covering pass/fail branches.
   - Demonstrate fail-closed behavior under malformed inputs.
2. **Lab GE-2: Replay Divergence Investigation**
   - Analyze replay mismatch artifacts.
   - Isolate deterministic contract violations.
   - Produce remediation patch plan and verification report.
3. **Lab GE-3: Evidence-Complete Release Exercise**
   - Execute a simulated change lifecycle.
   - Complete claims-evidence matrix row and gate outputs.
   - Validate release evidence completeness tooling output.

**Lab Pass Criteria**
- All mandatory tests passing
- No weakened/disabled governance tests
- Evidence bundle completeness = 100%

## Certification Level 3: Enterprise Administrator

### Candidate Profile
Enterprise Administrators own program-level governance outcomes, operational resilience, and multi-team enablement across enterprise environments.

### Learning Objectives
By the end of preparation, candidates should be able to:

1. Govern enterprise-wide ADAAD deployments across business units and regulated workloads.
2. Define role-based access, segregation-of-duties, and governance delegation models.
3. Manage upgrade, incident, and compliance programs with measurable control effectiveness.
4. Interpret governance telemetry to drive reliability, risk, and audit posture improvements.
5. Lead cross-functional governance councils and executive reporting for policy decisions.

### Exam Blueprint
| Domain | Weight | Skills Measured |
|---|---:|---|
| Enterprise Governance Strategy | 20% | Operating models, policy lifecycle, board-level controls |
| Security, Risk & Compliance Operations | 25% | Control mapping, incident governance, regulatory alignment |
| Platform Administration & Reliability | 25% | Tenant strategy, lifecycle management, availability governance |
| Organizational Enablement & Delegation | 15% | RACI design, approval authorities, escalation chains |
| Metrics, Reporting & Continuous Improvement | 15% | KPI/KRI frameworks, maturity assessment, remediation planning |

**Assessment Format**
- 80 selected-response questions
- 1 case study analysis
- 180 minutes total
- Passing score: 80%
- Recommended prerequisite: Governance Engineer + 12 months enterprise administration responsibility

### Practical Labs
1. **Lab EA-1: Enterprise Rollout Simulation**
   - Design phased rollout across multiple business units.
   - Define governance checkpoints, success criteria, and rollback protocol.
   - Produce executive-ready deployment decision brief.
2. **Lab EA-2: Incident Governance Command Exercise**
   - Coordinate simulated high-severity governance incident response.
   - Direct containment, communication, and evidence preservation.
   - Deliver after-action plan with prioritized corrective actions.
3. **Lab EA-3: Compliance and Audit Readiness Review**
   - Evaluate control implementation against audit objectives.
   - Identify material gaps and remediation windows.
   - Finalize audit packet with sign-off workflow.

**Lab Pass Criteria**
- Governance decision quality >= 85/100 rubric score
- Complete and internally consistent artifact set
- Executive communication artifacts accepted by assessor panel

## Badge Issuance Process

### Badge Types
- **Digital Completion Badge** (for each certification level)
- **Verified Credential Badge** (identity-verified + exam/lab validated)
- **Recertification Badge** (issued upon periodic renewal)

### Issuance Workflow
1. **Identity Verification**
   - Confirm legal identity and candidate profile.
   - Validate candidate agreement to certification code of conduct.
2. **Eligibility Check**
   - Confirm prerequisites and required experience.
   - Validate completion of mandatory training modules.
3. **Exam Result Validation**
   - Verify passing score and proctoring integrity.
   - Flag anomalies for review before issuance.
4. **Lab Evaluation Confirmation**
   - Confirm all practical labs met pass criteria.
   - Ensure artifacts are archived for audit retention period.
5. **Credential Approval Gate**
   - Certification authority reviews candidate packet.
   - Final decision logged with immutable issuance event.
6. **Badge Generation and Delivery**
   - Generate badge with credential ID, issue date, and expiration date.
   - Deliver via learner portal and signed notification email.
7. **Registry Publication**
   - Publish status to internal credential registry.
   - Expose verification endpoint for HR/compliance teams.

### Badge Metadata Standard
Each issued badge should include:
- Candidate name (or enterprise-approved identifier)
- Certification level
- Credential ID (globally unique)
- Date issued
- Expiration/renewal date
- Issuing authority
- Verification URL or QR-linked endpoint
- Revocation status field

### Renewal and Revocation
- **Validity period:** 24 months for all levels.
- **Renewal requirements:** condensed exam + policy delta module + one practical lab refresher.
- **Revocation triggers:** confirmed misconduct, fraud, severe policy violation, or administrative/legal directive.
- **Appeals process:** formal review within 30 days, with final determination recorded in credential ledger.

### Service-Level Targets
- Exam scoring turnaround: <= 5 business days
- Lab grading turnaround: <= 10 business days
- Badge issuance after full pass confirmation: <= 3 business days
- Revocation notice publication: <= 1 business day from final decision

## Program Governance and Quality Control

1. **Quarterly Blueprint Review** — ensure domain weighting reflects current platform and regulatory requirements.
2. **Semiannual Lab Refresh** — rotate scenarios to reduce answer memorization and align with evolving controls.
3. **Psychometric Monitoring** — evaluate item performance, discrimination indices, and fairness metrics.
4. **Assessor Calibration** — standardize grading quality using blind dual-review sampling.
5. **Audit Readiness** — maintain complete evidence for issuance decisions, changes, and appeals.

## Recommended Delivery Model
- Self-paced digital curriculum + instructor-led workshops
- Role-based learning paths mapped to job family
- Capstone simulations for Governance Engineer and Enterprise Administrator
- Manager dashboard for progress, readiness, and renewal risk
