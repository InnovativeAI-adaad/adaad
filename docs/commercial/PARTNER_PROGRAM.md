# ADAAD Partner Program

## Purpose
The ADAAD Partner Program defines the commercial and delivery model for organizations that resell, refer, implement, and co-deliver ADAAD capabilities for customers in regulated and high-assurance environments.

---

## 1) Partner Tiers

### Registered Partner
**Profile:** Early-stage referral and implementation partners onboarding to ADAAD.

**Eligibility baseline:**
- Signed Partner Agreement and NDA.
- Company profile, tax information, and billing setup complete.
- At least one named technical and one named commercial point of contact.

**Benefits:**
- Access to standard sales collateral and product overview materials.
- Access to onboarding kit templates.
- Referral compensation eligibility.

### Select Partner
**Profile:** Active partners with repeat pipeline and delivery readiness.

**Eligibility baseline:**
- Registered Partner requirements satisfied.
- Minimum quarterly pipeline submission and activity cadence met.
- At least two certified individuals (one technical, one delivery/governance).
- At least one approved customer implementation package.

**Benefits:**
- Increased referral/revenue-share rates.
- Deal registration support and solution engineering office hours.
- Co-selling and co-marketing eligibility.

### Premier Partner
**Profile:** High-performing strategic partners with proven co-delivery quality.

**Eligibility baseline:**
- Select Partner requirements satisfied.
- Multi-quarter performance targets met (bookings, deployment quality, retention).
- At least four certified individuals across technical, delivery, and governance tracks.
- Evidence of successful co-delivery under ADAAD standards.

**Benefits:**
- Highest referral/revenue-share rates.
- Priority access to roadmap briefings and launch programs.
- Joint account planning and executive sponsorship.

---

## 2) Enablement Requirements

### Commercial enablement
- Complete partner onboarding workshop (program model, positioning, qualification).
- Demonstrate ability to scope opportunities with the ADAAD discovery framework.
- Adopt approved proposal and statement-of-work template language.

### Technical enablement
- Complete implementation bootcamp (architecture, security, determinism, operations).
- Demonstrate baseline environment setup and deployment runbook competence.
- Submit at least one sample implementation package for review.

### Governance enablement
- Complete governance and evidence training.
- Demonstrate understanding of fail-closed controls, release evidence expectations, and audit readiness requirements.
- Pass governance checkpoint review before first production co-delivery.

### Ongoing enablement cadence
- Quarterly release update brief attendance.
- Annual recertification for at least one technical and one governance specialist.
- Participation in quality review for active co-delivery engagements.

## 3) Referral and Revenue-Share Model

### Referral model
- Partner submits qualified opportunities through partner registration workflow.
- Referral qualifies when account is net-new or partner-influenced and accepted by ADAAD commercial operations.
- Referral fee is payable only after customer contract execution and first paid invoice.

### Revenue-share model
- Revenue share applies to approved co-sell/co-delivery opportunities with documented partner scope.
- Share percentage is tier-based and finalized in the executed Partner Agreement addendum.
- Revenue share may include onboarding, implementation, managed services, or expansion deals as defined per agreement.

### Tier-based economics framework
| Tier | Referral economics | Revenue share range* | Payment condition |
|---|---|---|---|
| Registered | Entry-level referral fee | 5%–10% | Customer invoice paid |
| Select | Enhanced referral fee | 10%–20% | Customer invoice paid + acceptance criteria met |
| Premier | Strategic referral fee | 15%–30% | Customer invoice paid + governance acceptance complete |

\* Final percentages are defined in executed commercial terms and may vary by region, channel type, and service mix.

### Controls and exclusions
- No double-compensation across overlapping partner claims unless expressly approved.
- Deals involving non-compliant delivery can be placed on payment hold pending remediation.
- Disputed attribution follows documented deal registration precedence and timestamped evidence.

## 4) Co-Delivery Standards

All partner-led or joint-delivery engagements must adhere to the following standards:

1. **Scope clarity:** Customer scope, success criteria, and acceptance tests defined before execution.
2. **Governance readiness:** Delivery plan includes governance controls, evidence production, and audit checkpoints.
3. **Security baseline:** Partner follows ADAAD security and deployment hardening guidance for agreed environments.
4. **Deterministic operations:** Implementation avoids non-deterministic behavior in governed paths without explicit approval.
5. **Evidence completeness:** Required release and deployment evidence delivered prior to customer sign-off.
6. **Escalation discipline:** Incidents and blockers raised through defined support/escalation channels.
7. **Change control:** Material architecture or policy changes require documented review and approval.
8. **Post-go-live handoff:** Operational runbooks, ownership matrix, and support model agreed at handover.

### Co-delivery quality gates
- Architecture and security design review passed.
- Implementation package approved before production cutover.
- Acceptance report signed by customer and partner delivery lead.
- Post-implementation review completed within 30 days of go-live.

## 5) Certification Exam Criteria

### Exam tracks
1. **Commercial Specialist**
2. **Technical Implementer**
3. **Governance & Assurance Specialist**

### Exam structure
- **Format:** 60% objective questions, 40% scenario-based responses.
- **Duration:** 120 minutes per track.
- **Passing score:** 80% minimum overall and at least 70% in each domain.
- **Validity period:** 12 months from pass date.

### Domain weighting
| Track | Domain | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| Commercial Specialist | Qualification, positioning, pricing/commercial model | 100% |
| Technical Implementer | Architecture, deployment, operations, troubleshooting | 100% |
| Governance & Assurance Specialist | Controls, evidence, compliance, incident response | 100% |

### Practical evaluation criteria
- Ability to produce compliant implementation artifacts.
- Ability to explain and apply governance controls in customer scenarios.
- Ability to identify risk conditions and prescribe remediation paths.

### Recertification
- Annual retest or approved continuing-education credits.
- Partners with lapsed certifications may lose tier benefits until recertified.
